The former home page of the RPI Electronics Club

What happened to the RPI Electronics Club?

The RPI Electronics Club was shut down during the Summer of 2010. This domain name passed through several hands, looking for a home, until it was finally reregistered by Kenley Cheung after the Embedded Hardware Club let it expire in 2013. This website now provides information and resources about student-led software and hardware engineering research and development efforts at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.

What are some similar clubs and organizations?

  • The Embedded Hardware Club is a Union-funded club dedicated to electronics development and can be considered to be the spiritual successor to the EClub. The name could use some work.
  • The Student Senate Web Techologies Group is an agency of the Senate that is responsible for the development and maintainence of several on-campus open-source projects. They have worked with the EClub and RPI TV on the Hockey Line Sign.
  • RPI TV is the student-run television station for Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. They provide TV production and web streaming services to campus organizations and film lots of events around campus, including hockey games, football games, news programs, cultural performances, and the GM Week Debates. The Research and Development arm of RPI TV develops open-source graphics, scoreboard, and tiling and character generation software used for their state-of-the-art productions. They also collaborated with the WTG and EClub on the Hockey Line Sign.
  • RCOS (Rensselaer Center for Open Source Software) is a research program under the RPI Computer Science department that supports undergraduate research in open-source software. RCOS has supported students financially (with $$) and academically (with academic credit) when developing open-source projects. They have provided support for various projects created by the organizations listed above.
  • TVCOG (Tech Valley Center of Gravity) is a makerspace in downtown Troy.
